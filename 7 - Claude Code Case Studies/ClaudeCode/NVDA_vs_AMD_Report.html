NVIDIA (NVDA)

The Undisputed AI Infrastructure King

NVIDIA closed FY2026 (Jan 25, 2026) with record revenue of $215.9B (+65% YoY) and Q4 revenue of $68.1B (+73% YoY), crushing estimates. Data Center, now 91% of revenue at $62.3B in Q4, grew 75% YoY. Q1 FY2027 guidance of $78B smashed the $72.6B analyst consensus by 7.4%. Blackwell GPU cloud capacity is completely sold out with a $500B booking pipeline through 2026. The stock trades at $184.89 with a ~$4.37T market cap. Wall Street's 37 analysts rate it Strong Buy, with an average price target of $272, implying ~47% upside. NVIDIA's CUDA software moat and GB300 dominance (now 2/3 of Blackwell revenue) make it extremely difficult to displace in the near term.

AMD (AMD)

Strong Fundamentals, Recovering from a Guidance Shock

AMD reported record FY2025 revenue of $34.6B (+34% YoY) with Q4 2025 revenue of $10.27B (+34% YoY), beating estimates. However, Q1 2026 guidance of $9.8B — a sequential decline — sparked a 17% single-day crash on Feb 4, 2026. AMD trades at $200.21 with a ~$332B market cap. Forward P/E of ~34x vs NVIDIA's ~24x means AMD trades at a premium valuation despite lower growth. The MI350 GPU shows promise — outperforming NVIDIA's B200 by 20–30% in certain workloads — and OpenAI has selected AMD as a preferred partner for H2 2026. The MI400 "Vulkan" launch in 2026 is the key catalyst. ROCm software ecosystem remains the major structural gap vs CUDA.

🏆

12-Month Verdict: NVIDIA is the Higher-Conviction Pick; AMD has Higher Upside Potential

Over the next 12 months, NVIDIA is the lower-risk, stronger-fundamentals choice with $78B+ quarterly revenue guidance, a 47% analyst-implied upside, and a CUDA moat that will take years to erode. For investors who prioritize certainty of execution, NVDA is the clear winner. However, AMD presents an asymmetric opportunity: at a post-crash price of ~$200 with a 52-week low of $76.48, it has already compressed significantly from its $267 high. If MI400 launches on schedule, the OpenAI partnership converts to meaningful revenue, and AI capex continues at pace, AMD's analyst consensus target of ~$260 implies ~30% upside — with meaningful beat potential. NVIDIA wins on scale and certainty. AMD wins if you believe in its underdog trajectory and want leveraged AI exposure at a comparatively lower absolute price.

⚠ Key Risks — NVDA: China export restrictions (no China revenue assumed in guidance), valuation at $4.37T, custom silicon threat from hyperscalers. AMD: CUDA moat durability, execution risk on MI400, guidance volatility. This is not financial advice.

Bull & Bear Cases

🟢 NVDA Bull Case

  • Blackwell cloud capacity completely sold out; $500B booking pipeline extends into 2026
  • Q1 FY2027 guidance of $78B beats consensus by ~7.4%, signaling continued demand acceleration
  • Vera Rubin architecture (2026/27) will extend leadership — GB300 already 2/3 of Blackwell revenue
  • Agentic AI inflection is driving orders-of-magnitude more inference compute demand (Jensen Huang)
  • Analysts see 47% upside; Street-high $352 target could be achieved on continued beats
  • Non-China revenue base actually reduces geopolitical risk concentration going forward

🔴 NVDA Bear Case

  • China export controls = $0 assumed in forward guidance; any escalation hurts further
  • $4.37T market cap — already the world's most valuable company, low margin for error
  • Custom silicon (Google TPUs, AWS Trainium) offers 30–44% lower TCO in inference workloads
  • Open-source AI models (e.g., DeepSeek) may reduce compute intensity per task
  • Power constraints could limit physical deployment even if demand stays high
  • Hardware obsolescence: each Blackwell generation makes prior-gen Hopper OpEx-obsolete

🟢 AMD Bull Case

  • MI355X outperforms NVIDIA B200 by 20–30% in DeepSeek/Llama inference; cost/token advantage
  • OpenAI selected AMD as preferred partner for H2 2026 — enormous credibility signal
  • MI400 "Vulkan" launching 2026: up to 10× MI355X performance, full-rack Helios architecture
  • EPYC server CPU continues taking share from Intel — diversified revenue floor
  • After –17% earnings-day crash, AMD is arguably the most mispriced AI chip stock right now
  • KeyBanc projects $14–15B AI GPU revenue for 2026 — still early innings vs NVIDIA scale

🔴 AMD Bear Case

  • CUDA moat is 15+ years of developer entrenchment — ROCm cannot replicate quickly
  • Sequential revenue decline guided for Q1 2026 despite "AI boom" narrative
  • TTM P/E of ~77× and Forward P/E ~34× = expensive relative to AMD's actual AI revenue base
  • Q1 2026 guidance of $9.8B was a positive surprise vs estimates but still spooked investors
  • AMD's ~10–14% AI GPU market share leaves it structurally dependent on NVIDIA's stumble
  • Gaming and Embedded segments remain volatile recovery stories with uncertain timing
